Labeling is when we let something negative define us. "I did x" becomes "I am an x-er." If we view an action as representative of a fundamental characteristic of us, then there is no reason to endeavor to change. However, when we confront this distortion and its hold on our self-conception, then we can change whatever is making the mistake occur. Labeling frequently affects relationships; we label our friends and family instead of viewing their actions in context and talking to them to bring about the desired changes. An extreme form of labeling is name-calling: labelling yourself not just as unintelligent but as an idiot, not just as undisciplined but as a lazy slob, etc.
