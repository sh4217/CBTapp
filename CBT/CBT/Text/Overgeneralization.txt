Overgeneralization is when we assume that something bad that happened once is guaranteed to happen over and over again. This causes us to inflate the importance and the negative feeling of the event, and to feel powerless and unwilling to put in the effort to make a good outcome likelier next time. Dr. Burns notes that "the pain of rejection is generated almost entirely from overgeneralization. In its absence, a personal affront is temporarily disappointing but cannot be seriously disturbing." A classic example of overgeneralization in this vein is someone who is declined upon asking another person out on a date and concludes that nobody will ever want them and that they are guaranteed to be alone forever. Not only will this make them feel badly in the present, it can become a self-fulfilling prophecy by draining them of the motivation to meet people in the future.
