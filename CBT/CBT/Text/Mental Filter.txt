A mental filter causes a person to focus exclusively on the negative aspect of a situation while completely ignoring the positive aspects of the situation. Your vision becomes gray, cloudy, and unwilling to entertain the positive aspects of the situation. The mental filter is one of the hallmarks of depression. For example, someone returning from a successful date might spend all of their time thinking about the one joke they told that their date didn't laugh at. Conversely, one bad experience with someone from a group of people could lead to the distortion that the entire group of people is bad.
