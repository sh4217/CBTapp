Should statements are an excessive focus on obligation: "I should do this, I must do that." Albert Ellis calls this distortion "musturbation." These statements can generate unnecessary distress. Should statements cause the inevitable distance between our standards and our reality to be viewed as evidence of personal failing. Instead of the self-flagellation of "I have to do x or I'm a weak person," it can be helpful to reframe these thoughts as "I would benefit from x in y concrete way and feel happier as a result."
