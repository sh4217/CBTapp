Magnification is when we blow our faults or our challenges out of proportion, and minimization is when we pretend that our virtues or successes are microscopic. Magnification is also commonly referred to as catastrophizing. For example, a college student engages in magnification when she views a failed test as not just a failed test, but evidence that she is going to fail the class, and flunk out of college, and have zero chance of attaining her desired career. Another common manifestation is hypochondria: someone has an anxiety attack because they assume that their headache is evidence of a brain tumor.
