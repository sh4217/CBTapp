This occurs when we automatically assume a negative interpretation of a situation that is not justified by the facts. Dr. Burns identifies two main forms of this distortion. Mind reading is when we assume that people think poorly of us without them having said so, without actually asking them, or refusing to believe them when we ask and they say that they don't have these negative opinions. This can also become a self-fulfilling prophecy by causing us to withdraw from our friends or respond with an unkindness equal to that which we have mistakenly perceived. The other major form is the fortune teller error, when we assume something negative is guaranteed to occur, even if this assumption is unrealistic. One of the commonest manifestations of this distortion is people who just "know" that life will never get better and that they'll be depressed forever.
