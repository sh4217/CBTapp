This occurs when you evaluate your personal qualities or your life events in an extreme, black-or-white manner. For example, a tennis player might tell himself "I'm a terrible tennis player!" after losing a single match. Likewise, black-or-white thinking might lead a person to view one piece of criticism from a friend as evidence of that friend's hatred. This distortion can be related to perfectionism: any sort of mistake or deviation from an ideal is viewed as intolerable, and can lead a person to be too fearful to undertake a worthwhile activity. As Dr. David Burns notes, "Absolutes do not exist in this universe. If you try to force your experiences into absolute categories, you will be constantly depressed because your perceptions will not conform to reality."
