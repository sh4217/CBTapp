Personalization causes us to take responsibility for a negative event that we did not cause. Dr. Burns describes it as "the mother of guilt." Many people in abusive relationships personalize the abuse by saying that it is their fault that it occurs, even though this could not be further from the truth. In a less extreme fashion, a parent might personalize the matter of a child who isn't doing well in college, but the onus is ultimately on the child to take responsibility for their own success. Personalization can be one of the most demoralizing distortions, because you internalize negative sentiment over a situation that you, by definition, cannot affect. It is vital to learn to loosen your personal attachment to situations that you cannot control.
