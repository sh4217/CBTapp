Disqualifying the positive is an even more extreme version of the mental filter. Instead of merely ignoring the positive details of a situation, we actively reframe them to have neutral-to-negative explanations! For example, a person might hear an honest compliment and conclude that the person is either sucking up or even secretly mocking them. Or, when our friends remind us of a notable success, we reflexively respond, "It was just a fluke." Dr. Burns compares this distortion to a bad scientist who is set on proving a pet hypothesis about what a second-rate person he is. Any evidence supporting this hypothesis is put on a pedestal, whereas the evidence disproving it is thrown in the trash.
